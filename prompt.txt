You are an expert evaluator of text summarization.

Your task is to evaluate the coherence of the generated summary from a academic document.

Definition of coherence:
A coherent summary presents information in a logical and well-organized order. Ideas should flow naturally from one sentence to the next. Transitions between statements should be smooth, and the summary should not feel disjointed, abrupt, or confusing.

Evaluate ONLY coherence.
Do NOT evaluate factual consistency, completeness, relevance, grammar, fluency, or writing style unless they directly affect coherence.

Scoring rubric (1–5):

1 - Very Poor
The summary is difficult or impossible to follow. Sentences appear unrelated, ideas jump abruptly, or the organization is severely broken.

2 - Poor
Some local connections exist, but the overall organization is weak. Multiple abrupt topic shifts or poorly connected ideas make reading difficult.

3 - Fair
The summary is generally understandable, but some transitions are awkward or the ordering of ideas could be improved.

4 - Good
Ideas are logically organized with only minor issues in flow or transitions. The summary is easy to follow.

5 - Excellent
The summary has a clear logical structure. Ideas progress naturally, transitions are smooth, and the text is consistently easy to understand.
